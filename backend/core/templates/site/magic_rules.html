Frequent Rules

Quick references for Commander, the layer system, and the stack.
Commander Quick Rules 2 groups
Deck Construction Rules
Players choose a legendary creature as the commander for their deck.
A card’s color identity is its color plus any mana symbols in its rules text; it’s fixed before the game.
Cards in a deck may not include any colors outside the commander’s color identity.
Commander decks contain exactly 100 cards including the commander (companion is a 101st card if used).
Except basic lands, no two cards share the same English name unless a card says otherwise.
Play Rules
Players begin the game with 40 life.
Commanders start in the command zone; casting again costs an additional {2} each time.
If a commander would go to the graveyard or exile, its owner may move it to the command zone.
If a commander would go to hand or library, its owner may put it into the command zone instead.
Commander status is a property of the physical card (CR 109.3), not a copyable characteristic.
A player dealt 21 combat damage by a single commander loses the game.
Layer System (CR 613) 7 layers
1. Copy effects
Copiable values and face-down changes.
2. Control-changing effects
Who controls the object.
3. Text-changing effects
Rules text edits and swaps.
4. Type-changing effects
Card type, subtype, supertype.
5. Color-changing effects
Color overrides and additions.
6. Ability add/remove
Abilities gained/lost, keyword counters.
7. Power/Toughness
7a: Characteristic-defining abilities that set P/T.
7b: Effects that set P/T to a specific value.
7c: Modifiers and counters that change P/T.
7d: Effects that switch power and toughness.
The Stack (CR 405) 5 steps
1. Put on the stack
Casting a spell or activating/triggering an ability adds it on top.
2. Last in, first out
The most recent object resolves first when everyone passes priority.
3. APNAP ordering
Simultaneous additions go active player first, then others in turn order.
4. What’s on the stack
Spells use card characteristics; abilities use their text and controller.
5. What bypasses it
Static abilities, mana abilities, special actions, turn-based and state-based actions.
